Ruven Gottlieb <igadget at earthlink.net> writes:

> 1. If you use Varkon, what do you do with it?

Well, not yet.

> 2. If you don't use it, why not?

Well. I'm into designing very detailed and high precision
infrastructure, like roads going up mountains, playing fields, parks,
etc, very organic stuff, but very exact engineering and I can't really
model anything without doing the initial design interactively.

> 3. How does Varkon compare in any way with commercial offerings?

The most crucial tools for me, to get anything done, are: 

* Interactively draw and modify splines, then later use commands to
  analyze and correct them to make them perfect and simple (CV and EP
  spline tools)
* Detach/Attach splines at chosen point and rebuild uniformly
* Smooth/simplify splines
* Snap to arbitrary point on splines and end points
* Find intersection of splines
* Create offset splines
* Create surfaces from 4 splines (loft)
* Knot insertion
* Duplicate/extrude profile along spline
* Attach/detach surfaces
* Increase/decrease isoparms on surfaces and rebuild uniformly
* Use a background image as I base my models on flight photographs
* Extensive group management, cause I usually end up with a couple of
  thousand splines and a couple of hundred NURBS surfaces

> 4. What does Varkon need to make it more usable or better?

Total separation of core and UI; not just library separation. Make the
core a daemon that you connect a UI to, like a GTK toolbar;) and
arbitrary number of views using MVC/three tier architecture. Have a
REPL of some kind to take commands; an emacs mode;).

Btw, why is there no cooperation with BRL-CAD?. Both are written in c
and both are aiming to be parametric and implement proper NURBS tools.
