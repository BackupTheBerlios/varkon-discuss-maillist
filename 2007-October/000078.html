 Hi Ruven, here goes.....


1. If you use Varkon, what do you do with it?
I have used it to produce detail drawings for steel fabrication workshops.

2. If you don't use it, why not?
I now have a job in management and the company has major investment in several commercial programs and in the training for those programs. May use it again later.

 3. How does Varkon compare in any way with commercial offerings?
It's very good at what it does, but for many it is difficult to see what it can do. It needs a good amount of programming to make it useful for anything complex. If the time is taken to develop a program, then it is possibly the fastest and easiest way to accomplish production CAD work of generically similar items.

4. What does Varkon need to make it more usable or better?
A step-through debugger would be one thing. It also appears that the compiler has some kind of an upper limit when the drawing gets very large - say about 2000 parts. I have had drawings where the compiler chops the last few parts off. May be this was just a limitation of the machine I was using, but all the GM/PM/RTS sizes being maxed out did not help. 
Possibly the biggest obstacle to general acceptance is the lack of a 'pretty' interface with interactive zoom, pan/ rotate/floating toolbars etc
More application-specfic (i.e. mechanical, electrical, architecture etc...)  demo applications with tutorials are required.
