I report some ideas I had reading the post.

--- Ruven Gottlieb <igadget at earthlink.net> wrote:
> What we need is functionality. As one of the other
> respondents said 
> "interactive zoom, pan/ rotate/floating toolbars
> etc". I don't have any 
> problem with Varkon's menu system per se, I have a
> problem with lack of 
> functionality where it exists.

I think the main problem is the interactive use. I am
an architect, and models I use are often very
different from each other. The usual approach is to
modify interactively the drawing adding or modifying
elements, but Varkon requires too much time to make
this.

> Especially in the case of an explicit CAD app, the
> UI is everything: How 
> do you move entities or groups? Change entities,
> attributes? We have to 
> include everything we need to have, without putting
> in things we don't 
> need. That sounds like another reason to make the UI
> customizable or 
> modular, so people can choose.

An alternative is to have a core engine and different
frontends created for different purposes
(architecture, mechanics etc). Frontends could
interface with the engine through documented API.

I hope this was useful.
