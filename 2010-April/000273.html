Hi all,

My name is Jonathan York, and I am the person that Mr. Macphail talked about
who would do the packaging for Ubuntu. I am going to focus my attention on
packaging the latest version of Varkon for Ubuntu 10.04 which releases later
today. I am in the process of becoming a Debian/Ubuntu official maintainer
for varkon as (to them) it is an orphaned project and on the verge of
getting dropped from repo's.

My first step is to create a .deb that will install varkon with little to no
troubles. I have read on these mailing lists of several issues. Those who
have encountered these issues, can you please send me the details at my
email address? My first job as official maintainer is to revamp the
description of it to the world, and reclassify it as LGPL and explain
briefly what Varkon is.

Not trying to rock the boat too much here, but instead of having a lengthy
man page(s), why don't we set up a wiki that the man page can refer too,
like that anyone can create a help/tutorial with ease and make it available
to everyone.

With the all work and contributions going into Varkon these days, I see its
future as bright and shiny. Im a beginner/intermediate programmer and might
not be able to help very much in developping it further, but I am an
experienced ubuntu developper and I look forward to making Varkon
accessible, and easy to learn/use

If you have any questions/comments, lets hear them

Thanks

Jon York
