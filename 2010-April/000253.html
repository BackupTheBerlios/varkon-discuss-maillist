Hello Johan and John and all others,

eventually stumbling into this.
This week I was strugling with my laptop, after I had installed
GTK 2.18 (?) under /usr/local/ since the printed circuit CAD
program "pcb" wouldn't compile with the dated version provided
by Debian "etch".
>From this moment (as I realized on next boot and understood
after almost a week of partly fighting with X and Gnome settings
study) all the fonts on the Gnome desktop, including window
decorations, titles, certain buttons on popups etc. displayed
only littel boxes instead of character glyphs. No sending of
mail, no sensible storage of attachments, no controll over the
desktop settings, since all those menues were unreadable
as well of course... (shell windows and the content of mails
in Thunderbird was readable)

To remedy the problem, I upgraded to "lenny", result:
little boxes
In the end I moved /usr/local/bin, /usr/local/lib and /usr/local/share
to something else, to get the self-compiled GTK+ out of any path.
Voila, text repeared on my desktop - this was a really depressing
week, where I got only a fraction of my professional work under
way, not to mention CAD-demo or Varkon.

Positive side effects: I learned a cool way, to backup directory trees
containing recursive soft links to another machine with cpio and netcat,
can change some settings of the desktop like wall paper with gconftool-2 
now, learned about resolvconf(8) and fixed a long standing problem with 
switching
network setups and /etc/resolv.conf.

Best Wishes, Armin
