I'm not the main author of Varkon and I have to admit that I've become lazy

www.varkon.org

for you, you get the click,click,click,... behaviour for polylines e.g.

library: I use the original NVidia implementation forced into Debian.

Personally I so far had hardly any trouble compiling varkon for me.
Then again, I may be seasoned a programmer and fixing a header or
compiler switch on the fly counts as "easy" to me.

Thank you for your clear descriptions and constructive approach.
It adds some motivation to my efforts, that I've been lacking
badly in the last time.

> On the dream side:  It would be nice to be able to place a raster (a 
> picture) in the background and trace over it.  Even better would be 
> for it to place the raster in real world size and place by reading a 
> TIFF World File.   In case you want to add such a thing, the .tfw 
> placement X,Y is based on UpperLeftCorner of MostNorthwestPixel. Other 
> raster images sometimes use Center of MostNorthwestPixel.  So being 
> able to shift incoming raster needs to be accounted for.  Size (scale 
> of image) is simply accomplished
> by multiplying Xscale and Yscale values times pixel column/line value 
> to get upper left placement position of pixel.  Pixel offset is zero 
> based.  Upper left pixel is 0,0.  Upper LeftGround position is X + (0 
> x pixelXwidth), Y + (0 x pixelYheight).  Thus a point 5 columns right 
> and 14 lines down would be located at:
> X + (5 x pixelXwidth), Y + (14 x pixelYheight).
>
> example:
> contents of sac-napa.tfw
> 20.000000000000                   Ground length of X
> 0.000000000000                     aways 0.0
> 0.000000000000                     always 0.0
> -20.000000000000                  Ground length of Y (always '-' and 
> =X for square)
> 298081.592832696270            Easting      (X)
> 4287483.560435335200          Northing   (Y)
>
> 6 lines, no comments, just the left side, left set.  Units 
> (projection, meters,  feet,  Lat/Long, ...) are assumed to be known 
> and within the file are all of same measure and origin.
> The two 0.0 lines are for use in a matrix,  one is scale and the other 
> is rotation.  I have never seen them be anything but Zero in actual 
> day to day use.  They would be used if you were to be handling raw 
> satellite output because capture is skewed and orbits are not 
> perfectly circular around center of Earth.
