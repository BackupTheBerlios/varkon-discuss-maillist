> > 4) is it possible to export geometries in STL format?
>
> I don't know this; looking at the description of STL in wikipedia, given
> a u,v-surface,
> generation of triangle-strips with no connectivity should be trivial
>
> > 5) if a join a wing to a fuselage, can I use some sort of boolean
> > operation to cut the part of the wing which is inside the fuselage
> > (surface to surface intersection?
>
> This is work in progress; I'm implementing an intersection algorithm
> (surface-surface
> is done, handling of edges not implemented) and Varkon now has the
> ability to
> express trimmed surfaces to hold the result. Dr. Larsen is working on
> intersections
> as well from what I know. This part of my work is not yet integrated
> with Varkon.
>
> The result of such an intersection will be a pair (or set) of trimmed
> surfaces, where
> the trim-lines are a high quality approximation of the true intersection
> curve. As they
> are expressed as splines in u,v-space on a given surface, isolines can
> again be used
> to generate triangle strips for conversion to STL. Of course
> improvements like
> adaptive spacing are possible - it's open source and you are welcome to
> help ;-)
> The implementation of an STL-generator could be done in MBS or C - I'm
> not sure
> which is better - with respect to my GUI-demo it could be C++ as well.
>
> As I am interested in CFD applications myself, time permitting I may
> help with this.
>
