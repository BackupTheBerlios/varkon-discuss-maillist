Armin, and all others:

For me it is wonderful that Johan is moving Varkon to GTK+.

(A) This means quick access to a rich widget set. Tree
widgets are useful in many applications, and perhaps can
be used in Varkon itself such as for browsing the GM.
Tabbed windows become important for larger programs,
especially for dialogs presenting a lot of settings. So
there are usability benefits, it's not just eye candy.

(B) Using standardized widgets should make it easy
to develop other programs to work with Varkon and
present a seamless appearance.

(C) A familiar appearance will make it easier to get
more Varkon users.

(D) It feels much safer that someone with deep
knowledge of Varkon is working on the new GUI,
so that Varkon becomes polished and enhanced
rather than risking having any of its existing strengths
obscured.

(E) Even though GTK+ like any software is hardly
likely to be perfect, it is at least written in C,
beautiful to look at, and successfully used by other
programs such as Inkscape.

So I hope this porting to GTK+ will get less criticism
and more support.

John (MacPhail)
