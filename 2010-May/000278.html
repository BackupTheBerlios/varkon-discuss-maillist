> About editing subparts: I assume you mean not editing the subpart 
> itself but
>
> editing the call to the subpart, ie. the parameters of the subpart. As 
> an example,
>
> let A be the currently active module. B is a part called by A and C is 
> a subpart called by B.
>
> Remember that B may be called more than once in A and that C may be 
> called more than
>
> once in B. The EditP function makes it possible to edit the parameters 
> of a part call in
>
> the currently active model. That means editing the paremeter values 
> that are passed to
>
> B from A. If A is the active module, you can not edit the part 
> parameters passed to C from B
>
> since this would not make it possible to reexecute A and get the same 
> result.
>
>  
>
> It's all about Generic and Explicit modeling, or if you wish, modeling 
> with history/no history.
>
> What I said above is true for generic modeling. In explicit modeling, 
> it would be possible to
>
> edit calls to subparts, at least in theory but that possibility is not 
> implemented in Varkon.
>
>  
>
> About editing "invisible" parts: People have solved this in many 
> different ways.
>
>  
>
> - You can identify a part through it's ID. Press "i" when the cursor lits.
>
> - Write a macro that searches for the part ID in the DB.
>
> - Let the part create a text entry in a table on a blanked level. To 
> edit, unblank the level
>
>   and select the part in the table.
>
> and so on....
>
>  
>
