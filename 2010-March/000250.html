For me personally, it was a confusing surprise in the 6-minute tutorial
when "vi" was launched as the MBS editor. Not even recognizing that
I was seeing vi, I couldn't seem to edit the file.

Unless there is configurability that I'm missing, it seems the call to
"vi" in WP/src/wpedms.c might better be replaced by invoking a
simple script.

My own workaround on Debian is partly clumsy and partly interesting.
I got an editor more familiar to me by creating $HOME/bin/vi as a
symbolic link to /usr/bin/sensible-editor, then got "nano", and
everything was fine.
