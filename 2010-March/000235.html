Hello John, Johan and all,

during my quest for a good widget library I was considering wxwidgets.
I don't know any longer, why exactly I ruled it out and opted for FLTK,
but look for the following:
*) how complicated is it, to deploy an executable on all possible platforms?
    need cygwin? -> then everyone wanting to use your app has to install 
cygwin as well
   -> no-go
   -> cool if it works out of the box with the MinGW-compiler under 
Windows, including
       the GL-demos
*) check very carefully, how the library handles OpenGL-drawing
   - everything in a wrapper? -> slow, limited -> no-go
   - do the demos run on every machine you get hold of?
     regretably FLTK assumes all machine have 24-bit visuals, but my 
laptop hasn't
     -> I'm trying to enhance FLTK now because of this
*) do you find the API clear and easy?
    - clumsy event handling? event propagation? grouping of widgets?

If I remember correct, the API of wx looked arkward to me and much of the
demos they offer didn't even compile everywhere. Take this with a grain 
of salt
since I did my search more than 4 years ago. Btw, I think wx looks just 
ugly.

If you want to have a look at my effort, there is a bit dated version on
www.varkon.org under GUI.

Btw. Johan, day before yesterday I got my demo working on the Athlon 64
again using the V1.18A libraries. I still have not understood the V1.19B
surface-interface without english toplevel-doku...

Sometimes, when I'm more bold than sensible I consider even writing a new
widget library - however, while I have some ideas for a fast, concise 
themable
lib, the effort is very high.

The reason, why I have not abandoned my stuff alltogether yet is, that 
some day
I want a free CAD app, that I enjoy to work with. I started out with the 
idea of
basing something comercial or dual-license on Varkon as well - do you have
2-3 fultime years to spend en-bloc John? What you see on www.varkon.org
resembles 3 months at 60h/week.

Best Regards, Armin
