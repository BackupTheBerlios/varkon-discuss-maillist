Hi Armin,

Thanks for the response.

The FLTK licensing is fascinating. The authors have
made huge changes to the LGPL, apparently with
the main objective of making static linking feasible.

I guess I had better expand on the LGPL issue.
Suppose

(a) You and I sell commercial program P statically
      linked to LGPL library V.

(b) Both the love of V and the spirit of free software
      catch on among our customers.

(c)  Each customer, busy tweaking V, invokes
      section 6(a) to require us to repeatedly ensure
      linking of P against each new version of V that the
      particular customer creates.

Perhaps (a)-(c) may seem far-fetched, but even a milder
version could be quite awkward.

Does my question, whether Varkon can feasibly move
to dynamic linking, begin to make more sense?

John (MacPhail)
