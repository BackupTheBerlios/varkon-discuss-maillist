Hi again Johan,

1. Sorry that my REF/XREF question is vague. I'm thinking of
the kind of situation where Document A describes an aircraft,
and Document E describes an aircraft engine. What remains
unclear to me is whether it would be convenient in Varkon to
maintain the documents separately and yet have A refer to E
so as to have images of the engine show up in images of the
aircraft. So I think I'm asking a fair question about potential
scalability to very complex models, but possibly I'm just not
properly grasping the REF usage.

2&3. As to what is wxWidgets (official answer is on this
page: http://www.wxwidgets.org/docs/faqgen.htm), it's a cross-
platform GUI toolkit, using native widgets, C++ oriented,
presented as an application framework, already in use by
some popular software such as Audacity and FileZilla.
Suggestion: consider building the main Varkon program with
wxWidgets, both for direct use and as an example for Varkon
applications. It might even be possible to persuade the Google
Summer of Code project to  finance work by a student on this.

John
